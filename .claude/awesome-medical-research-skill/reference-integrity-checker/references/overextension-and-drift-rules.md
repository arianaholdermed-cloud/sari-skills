# Overextension and Drift Rules

## Core Rule
Do not let manuscript wording exceed the actual scope of the cited source.

## Common Problems
- association described as causation
- animal or in vitro evidence generalized to clinical certainty
- one population generalized to a broader population
- exploratory trend written as established fact
- paraphrase stronger than source wording

## Important Rule
Quote drift can occur even when the citation is on-topic.

## Reporting Rule
State how the manuscript wording should be narrowed when the source support is overstretched.
