# Validation and Evidence Hierarchy
# faers-pharmacovigilance-disproportionality-research-planner

---

## Evidence Tiers

### Reporting-Signal Evidence (supportive — label explicitly as reporting signal)

| Evidence | Source | What It Establishes |
|---|---|---|
| Elevated ROR vs comparator | disproportionality analysis | Increased reporting signal relative to comparator |
| PT-level signal | MedDRA preferred-term comparison | Domain-specific signal pattern |
| Cross-drug signal contrast | within-class comparison | Relative reporting pattern, not true risk ranking |

### Comparator-Qualified Signal Evidence (stronger — label explicitly as comparator-qualified)

| Evidence | Source | What It Establishes |
|---|---|---|
| Signal persists across comparators | multiple comparator analysis | More stable reporting signal |
| Signal persists across indication groups | subgroup comparison | More structured pattern across strata |
| Strong-signal threshold reached | stricter ROR/CI rule | Higher-priority signal for follow-up |

### Signal-Priority Evidence (supportive — label explicitly as follow-up priority)

| Evidence | Source | What It Establishes |
|---|---|---|
| Severity grouping | PT-level clinical relevance grouping | Which signals may warrant earlier attention |
| Cross-product convergence | repeated signal across several products | Stronger follow-up interest |
| Biological plausibility layer | literature-supported context | Follow-up relevance, not causality |

---

## Language Rules

- Signal findings: "increased reporting signal", "disproportionately reported", "reporting pattern"
- Stronger findings: "comparator-qualified signal", "stronger follow-up priority"
- Follow-up findings: "warrants further investigation", "supports pharmacovigilance follow-up"

- **NEVER use:** "causes", "increases incidence", "confirms risk", "proves mechanism" from spontaneous-report disproportionality alone
