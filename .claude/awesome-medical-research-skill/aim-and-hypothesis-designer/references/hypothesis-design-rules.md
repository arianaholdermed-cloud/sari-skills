# Hypothesis Design Rules

Use this module to determine when a formal hypothesis is appropriate and how to write it.

A hypothesis is not a decorative sentence.
It should state a testable expectation that the study could genuinely assess.

## Valid Hypothesis Characteristics

A strong hypothesis should be:
- falsifiable,
- tied to a defined relation, effect, or directional expectation,
- linked to a real exposure / predictor / intervention / mechanism / comparison,
- and matched to the likely evidence type of the study.

## Do Not Use Formal Hypotheses For

- broad descriptive surveys,
- early discovery mapping,
- purely open-ended exploratory scans,
- aims where no clear comparison, association, or effect is being tested.

## Common Weak Forms

Avoid hypotheses that are:
- circular,
- obvious restatements of the aim,
- too broad to falsify,
- stronger than the design can support,
- causal when the evidence logic is observational only.

## Important Rule

When uncertain, prefer a clearly labeled exploratory objective over a falsely precise hypothesis.
