Use a cross-design comparison framework rather than a one-dimensional hierarchy.

When ranking papers, judge all of the following:
1. directness to the question being asked
2. inferential strength allowed by the design
3. methodological execution quality
4. validation depth
5. risk that conclusions exceed what the design can support

Suggested comparative language:
- high-weight anchor evidence
- strong supporting evidence
- moderate but context-dependent evidence
- exploratory or hypothesis-generating evidence
- low-weight or cautionary evidence

Do not force a universal ordering when papers answer different question types. Explain the comparison frame instead.
