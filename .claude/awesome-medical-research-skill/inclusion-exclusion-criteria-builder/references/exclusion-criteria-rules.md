# Exclusion Criteria Rules

## Core Rule
Exclusion criteria should remove records or participants only when they threaten interpretability, comparability, measurement validity, or protocol coherence.

## Common valid exclusion types
- wrong disease entity or uncertain diagnosis
- wrong time period or no valid index moment
- insufficient evidence to verify major eligibility features
- prior exposure that breaks the intended analytic contrast
- competing conditions that invalidate endpoint interpretation
- inadequate specimen quality or unavailable key assay material when central to the study

## Avoid
- reflexive overcleaning
- exclusions that merely remove complexity without scientific or operational justification
- exclusion rules that rely on vague reviewer judgment alone
