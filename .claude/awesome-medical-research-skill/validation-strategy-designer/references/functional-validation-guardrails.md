# Functional Validation Guardrails

## Core Rule
Do not design specific experiments from thin air.

## Before Proposing Functional Validation
Clarify:
- what biological claim is being tested,
- whether the claim is mechanistic, correlative, or predictive,
- which system is biologically relevant,
- what resources are available,
- whether wet-lab follow-up is actually feasible.

## Important Rule
If these are not known, do not fabricate:
- cell lines,
- animal models,
- organoids,
- perturbation methods,
- assays,
- readouts,
- timelines,
- expected effect directions.

## Reporting Rule
When evidence is incomplete, state that functional validation may be justified in principle, but specific experiments require further clarification.
