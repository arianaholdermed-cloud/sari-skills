# Design Family Selection Rules

## Core Rule
Pick one dominant planning family first. Do not mix formulas or logic families casually.

## Common Planning Families
- Two-group binary outcome
- Continuous outcome comparison
- Time-to-event / survival
- Case-control odds-ratio based planning
- Matched / paired design
- Prediction model development
- External validation
- Precision / confidence-interval width
- Fixed-N feasibility evaluation
- Clustered / repeated-measures design

## Important Distinctions
- Time-to-event studies are often event-driven, not purely N-driven.
- Prediction model planning is not the same as two-group hypothesis testing.
- External validation usually needs performance precision logic, not discovery logic.
- Case-control planning depends heavily on exposure prevalence and case-control ratio.
- Repeated-measures and clustered designs require inflation for dependence structure.

## Reporting Rule
Explicitly state why the chosen family is the correct primary driver.
