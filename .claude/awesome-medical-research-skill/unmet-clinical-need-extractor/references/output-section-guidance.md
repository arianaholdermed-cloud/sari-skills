# Output Section Guidance

Use this module to keep the final output clinically grounded, structured, and usable.

## Section Intent

### Section A — Clinical Need Framing
Define the exact need unit and the scope of extraction.

### Section B — Retrieval and Evidence Audit
Show what evidence sources were used and why they are relevant to unmet-need extraction.

### Section C — Patient-Journey Need Map
Show where current care breaks down or remains uncertain.

### Section D — Structured Unmet-Need Classification
Organize the unmet needs by type, stage, and evidence basis.

### Section E — True Pain Points vs Generic Importance Summary
Separate clinically meaningful unmet needs from broad but weak disease-value claims.

### Section F — Priority Unmet Clinical Needs
Identify the strongest clinically actionable unmet needs.

### Section G — Research-Value Translation
Show how the unmet need can justify a research direction without overclaiming.

### Section H — Most Actionable Framing Recommendation
Give the single strongest need anchor and the safest wording.

### Section I — Self-Critical Risk Review
Make overstatement risks and evidence limits visible.

### Section J — References
Provide only real, supportable sources.

## Reporting Rules

- Keep unmet-need statements specific and clinically operational.
- Make evidence strength visible.
- Use tables only when comparison is genuinely improved.
- Avoid vague “better treatment is needed” or “precision medicine is needed” phrasing.
- Keep translational framing downstream of validated need extraction.
