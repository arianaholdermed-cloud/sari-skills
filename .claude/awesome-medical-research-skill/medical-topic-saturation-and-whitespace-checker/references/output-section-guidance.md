# Output Section Guidance

Formatting standard for Sections A–I:
- Keep each section explicitly labeled.
- Prefer compact, decision-useful wording.
- Use tables as the default format for mapped sections whenever possible.
- Section C should foreground the saturation dimensions and the evidence supporting each one.
- Section E should present remaining whitespace as concrete entry angles, not vague ideas.
- Section F should tie timing judgment to evidence, not intuition.
- Section G must recommend one primary entry position or one clear non-entry conclusion.
- Section H must include self-critical review items, even when the field looks obviously crowded.

## Important Rule

Major claims about saturation, whitespace, or timing should be supported by data or evidence whenever possible.
Do not hide weak support behind confident wording.
