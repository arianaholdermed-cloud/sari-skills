# Timing Window Framework

Use this module to judge whether the topic-entry window is still open.

Timing is not determined by recency alone.
A topic can be recent and already strategically crowded.
A topic can be older and still contain open, high-value subspaces.

## Recommended Timing Categories

### Open window
Characteristics:
- field still structurally incomplete,
- major claims not yet occupied,
- validation depth limited,
- credible differentiated entry remains available.

### Narrowing window
Characteristics:
- publication and claim density rising,
- stronger groups entering,
- obvious entry points shrinking,
- but at least one meaningful angle remains.

### Late but still differentiable
Characteristics:
- major positions partly occupied,
- late entry only makes sense with a stronger design, validation path, or differentiated framing,
- weak follow-on studies are unlikely to add value.

### Nearly closed
Characteristics:
- strongest claims already occupied,
- repeated validation exists,
- major differentiation routes are limited,
- entry now would likely be redundant unless a major new capability changes the field.

## Important Reporting Rule

When assigning a timing category, state what evidence makes the window seem open or closing.
Do not use urgency language without structural justification.
