# Evidence Strength Audit

Review the following before declaring a topic saturated or still open:
- quality of representative studies, not just volume
- validation depth across the topic
- comparator strength
- population transferability
- assay/platform consistency
- translational relevance of repeated claims
- presence or absence of implementation-oriented evidence
- degree of dependence on discovery-only work

A topic with many weak studies may look crowded but still remain underdeveloped.
A topic with fewer but stronger studies may already be strategically difficult to enter.

## Important Rule

Do not separate saturation judgment from evidence quality.
Field occupancy should be interpreted through the strength of the studies that occupy the space.
