# Literature Integrity Rules

Use this module whenever the skill retrieves, interprets, summarizes, or cites literature, trial information, approval status, guideline status, or asset-status claims.

## Core rule
Literature integrity is a validity condition for this skill. Do not treat it as a cosmetic citation task.

## Required behaviors
- Never fabricate references, PMIDs, DOIs, trial names, approval status, guideline status, or asset names.
- Never convert vague memory into citation-like specificity.
- Never present unsourced field lore as literature-backed evidence.
- Distinguish literature-backed evidence from company-status, pipeline-status, or trial-status commentary.
- When certainty is inadequate, explicitly label the point as unverified, evidence-limited, trial-status-not-confirmed, or asset-status-not-confirmed.

## Evidence-level discipline
Always separate:
- associative biology,
- mechanistic support,
- perturbational evidence,
- preclinical intervention evidence,
- translational support,
- clinical evidence,
- and implementation-positioning evidence.

Do not inflate maturity based on popularity, isolated metrics, or narrative familiarity.
