# Literature Retrieval and Citation

Prioritize peer-reviewed reviews and original studies, then clinical sources when relevant.

Rules:
- Never invent papers, trials, approvals, PMIDs, DOIs, or named assets.
- Separate literature-backed claims from market commentary.
- Label uncertain points as `unverified` or `asset-status-not-confirmed`.
- Do not let citation-like phrasing hide weak evidence.
- Prefer concrete support for major claims about validation, failure, or clinical anchoring.
