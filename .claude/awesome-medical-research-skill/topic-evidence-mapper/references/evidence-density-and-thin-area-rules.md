# Evidence Density and Thin Area Rules

## Density Labels
Use one of the following:
- High density
- Moderate density
- Sparse
- Very sparse

## Interpretation Rule
Density is descriptive, not evaluative. A crowded area is not automatically the most important one, and a sparse area is not automatically a high-value opportunity.

## Thin Area Rule
Thin areas should be described as "thin" or "underdeveloped" rather than as formal research gaps unless a separate gap-analysis step is performed.
