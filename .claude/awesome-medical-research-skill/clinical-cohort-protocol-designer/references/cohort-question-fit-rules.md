# Cohort Question Fit Rules

Use a cohort design when the question requires temporal ordering from baseline to future outcome and the unit of analysis is a defined population under observation.

Good fit examples:
- prognosis or recurrence risk
- baseline predictor to later outcome
- observed treatment exposure to later effectiveness or safety outcome
- natural history or longitudinal disease burden

Important rule:
- A cohort design is not automatically the right choice for every observational question. If the event is rare, the exposure ascertainment is backward-looking, or the goal is purely diagnostic accuracy at one time point, consider whether case-control, cross-sectional, or diagnostic-design logic would fit better.

Interpretation rule:
- Always state whether the intended inference is descriptive, associative, predictive, or quasi-causal. Do not imply more than the design can support.
