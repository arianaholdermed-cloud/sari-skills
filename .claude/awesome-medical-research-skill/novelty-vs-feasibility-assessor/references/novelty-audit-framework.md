# Novelty Audit Framework

Assess novelty in five separate dimensions:

1. **Question novelty**
   - Is the scientific question materially different from what the field already asks?
2. **Context novelty**
   - Is the question being asked in a genuinely under-studied disease, population, stage, endpoint, or sample context?
3. **Method novelty**
   - Is there a real methodological shift, or just a new wrapper around familiar logic?
4. **Integration novelty**
   - Does cross-modal integration create new explanatory or predictive value, or just add complexity?
5. **Translation novelty**
   - Does the project move from association toward validation, utility, implementation, or decision relevance?

Also assess **pseudo-novelty risk**:
- same question, new algorithm label,
- same pipeline, new disease shell,
- stacked modalities without a sharper question,
- “first combination” claims without meaningful scientific gain.
