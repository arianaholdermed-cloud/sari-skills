# Database Selection Rules

Select databases by question type, not by habit.

## Core Defaults
- **PubMed**: primary biomedical database for medicine and life sciences
- **Google Scholar**: broad recall, citation chaining, grey-zone scholarly coverage
- **Web of Science**: citation-indexed coverage and broader disciplinary retrieval

## Add-on Databases When Justified
- **Embase**: stronger drug, pharmacology, and European journal coverage
- **Cochrane Library**: reviews and controlled-trial oriented clinical evidence
- **ClinicalTrials.gov**: trial registration and ongoing/completed trial lookup
- **bioRxiv / medRxiv / arXiv**: preprints and methods that may not yet be indexed elsewhere

## Selection Logic
Use at least 2–3 databases for most cross-database collection tasks.
Explain each choice with a concrete purpose.
Do not include a database just to make the list longer.

## Required Output Mapping
For each selected database, state:
- why it is included
- what it is expected to contribute
- what it is likely to miss
