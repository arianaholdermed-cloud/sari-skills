# Output Section Guidance

## Section A
Interpret the original idea rather than merely restating it.

## Section B
Classify the question type explicitly and name important secondary types.

## Section C
Make ambiguity visible; do not hide it.

## Section D
Explain why the chosen framing model fits better than common alternatives.

## Section E
Use a structured breakdown table with concrete element-level decisions.

## Section F
Provide three levels of reformulation: plain-language, research-ready, searchable.

## Section G
State both what is covered and what is intentionally excluded.

## Section H
Be honest about whether the question is currently searchable, researchable, and testable.

## Section I
Always recommend a next-step path.

## Section J
Warn the user how the question is most likely to be misframed.
