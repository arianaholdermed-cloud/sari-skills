# Ambiguity and Boundary Rules

A question is not clarified until its major ambiguities are visible.

## Common Missing Elements
- population or age group
- disease stage, subtype, or severity
- intervention / exposure definition
- comparator
- outcome definition
- timeframe
- care setting or biological context
- subgroup of interest
- intended evidence goal

## Common Boundary Failures
- topic is too broad to search effectively
- mechanism and clinical utility are mixed together
- association and causality are conflated
- diagnosis, prognosis, and prediction are treated as interchangeable
- the user wants multiple separate questions but states them as one

## Required Action
Always state:
- what is clear already
- what is being inferred
- what still needs narrowing
- what remains outside scope for now

## Scope-Reduction Rule
Prefer a smaller, answerable question over a grand but unusable formulation.
