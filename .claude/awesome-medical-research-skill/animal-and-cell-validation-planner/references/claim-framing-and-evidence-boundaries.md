# Claim Framing and Evidence Boundaries

Always define the exact claim first.

## Allowed claim styles
- “Target X contributes to phenotype Y under condition Z.”
- “Perturbing pathway A changes readout B in model context C.”
- “Drug D reverses or attenuates phenotype E in a disease-relevant model.”
- “Biomarker/signature F has functional support in model context G.”

## Boundary Rules
- Expression confirmation is not equal to causation.
- Perturbation effect is not equal to specificity unless specificity work is included.
- Cell evidence is not equal to organismal relevance.
- Animal evidence is not equal to clinical efficacy.
- Directional consistency with prior computational findings is support, not proof of mechanism completeness.

## Reporting Rule
In Section A and Section I, explicitly state:
- what the plan is trying to test,
- what it can support if successful,
- what it still cannot prove.
