# Model System Selection

Choose model systems by claim fit, not prestige.

## Cell-system selection logic
### Immortalized cell lines
Good for rapid perturbation and scalable first-pass testing.
Limitation: may poorly reflect disease context.

### Primary cells
Good when biological context matters more than throughput.
Limitation: variability and feasibility constraints.

### Patient-derived or organoid-like systems
Good for stronger disease relevance.
Limitation: complexity, access, and lower throughput.

### Co-culture / microenvironment-aware systems
Good when context dependence is central to the claim.
Limitation: interpretation complexity increases.

## Animal-system selection logic
### Xenograft-like systems
Useful for growth or treatment-response questions.
### Syngeneic / immune-competent contexts
Useful when immune context matters.
### Genetically relevant or phenotype models
Useful when disease mechanism or systemic phenotype matters.

## Core Rule
Only introduce animal work when it adds evidence that the cell system cannot provide.

## Feasibility Rule
If exact model availability is unknown, label the model as an example family, not a guaranteed available model.
