# Results Ordering Rules

## Core Rule
The Results section should be ordered by narrative and evidentiary function, not by the chronological order in which analyses were run.

## Typical Ordering Logic
- descriptive setup,
- data/sample characterization,
- primary findings,
- supportive mechanistic or cross-modal analyses,
- subgroup / sensitivity layers,
- validation results.

## Important Rule
Not every paper needs every block, and not every block deserves equal weight.

## Reporting Rule
Explain why the chosen order helps the reader understand the main claim more efficiently.
