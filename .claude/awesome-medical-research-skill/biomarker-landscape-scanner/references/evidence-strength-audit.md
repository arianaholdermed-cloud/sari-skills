# Evidence Strength Audit

Review the following:
- Cohort design: retrospective/prospective, case-control/cohort/trial-embedded
- Sample size adequacy
- Single-center vs multicenter
- Population representativeness
- Comparator quality: standard markers, clinical models, head-to-head benchmarks
- Assay reproducibility and standardization
- Threshold stability / cut-point definition
- Overfitting risk for signatures
- External validation presence and quality
- Clinical relevance of metrics used
- Incremental value beyond standard care variables

Strong evidence is not just a high AUC. It requires robustness, reproducibility, and clinically meaningful comparison.
