# Design Decision Rules

## Primary rule
Assign the design label from the main claim-carrying structure, not from peripheral validation layers.

## Decision priorities
1. Identify intervention vs non-intervention.
2. Identify temporal direction.
3. Identify grouping logic: by exposure, by outcome, or by simultaneous measurement.
4. Identify whether the main evidence comes from patients, datasets, models, cells, or animals.
5. Identify whether a second design layer is central enough to require a hybrid label.

## Hybrid rule
Use a hybrid label when two or more evidence layers are central to the paper's main conclusion. Do not use a hybrid label when a secondary layer is only decorative or confirmatory.

## Mislabel correction rule
If the author label conflicts with the structural logic, correct the design openly.
