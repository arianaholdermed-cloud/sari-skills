# Meaningful vs Cosmetic Stratification Rules

Use this module to decide whether a subgroup distinction is a real research opportunity or just superficial slicing.

## Meaningful Stratification
Treat a subgroup gap as meaningful when it could plausibly affect one or more of the following:
- disease biology
- diagnosis
- prognosis
- treatment response
- risk modeling
- implementation
- transportability of findings

## Cosmetic Stratification
Treat a subgroup split as cosmetic when it adds little biological, clinical, or interpretive value and mainly creates the appearance of precision.

## Important Rule
Do not upgrade a subgroup gap merely because the subgroup exists. The subgroup distinction must matter.
