# Reference Module 03: Matching and Exposure Ascertainment

## Matching Rule
Matching is optional, not mandatory.

Use matching only when it clearly improves comparability on major design-driving variables and does not erase meaningful exposure contrast.

## Matching Types To Distinguish
- no matching,
- individual matching,
- frequency matching.

## Do Not Match On
- likely mediators,
- variables too close to the exposure mechanism,
- variables that create overmatching,
- variables that unnecessarily restrict eligible controls.

## Overmatching Warning
Do not recommend broad or reflexive matching. Overmatching can reduce efficiency, distort exposure contrast, and complicate interpretation.

## Exposure Ascertainment Rule
The output must define:
- target exposure or predictor,
- exposure window,
- data source,
- whether exposure clearly precedes outcome,
- whether ascertainment differs between cases and controls.

## Recall Bias Rule
When exposure depends on interviews, memory, or subjective recall, the design must explicitly discuss recall bias and mitigation.

## Biomarker Timing Rule
A biomarker measured after diagnosis, hospitalization, complication onset, or treatment initiation should not be presented as a clean pre-outcome etiologic exposure unless the role is explicitly redefined.

## Reporting Rule
The output must explain:
- whether matching is recommended,
- what variables would be matched if matching is used,
- why the exposure measurement strategy is temporally credible or limited,
- and what the main timing distortion risk is.
