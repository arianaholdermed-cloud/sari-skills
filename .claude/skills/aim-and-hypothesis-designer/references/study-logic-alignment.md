# Study Logic Alignment

Use this module to align each aim with the actual evidence logic required.

## Alignment Questions

For each aim, specify:
- what must be observed or measured,
- what design logic is required,
- what minimum comparator or outcome structure is needed,
- what validation or replication level matters,
- and what hidden prerequisite could collapse the aim.

## Common Misalignments

- causal wording in purely observational framing,
- predictive claims without proper validation,
- mechanism claims without mechanistic evidence,
- translational language without a bridge to application,
- biomarker claims without clear utility context.

## Reporting Rule

If the likely study cannot generate the minimum evidence required for the aim’s wording, narrow the aim before finalizing.
