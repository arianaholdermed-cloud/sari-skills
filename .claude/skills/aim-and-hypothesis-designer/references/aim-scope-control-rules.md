# Aim Scope Control Rules

Use this module to prevent aim sprawl and incoherent scope.

## Common Scope Inflation Signals

- too many aims for one study unit,
- multiple unrelated evidence chains,
- separate populations or platforms with no unifying story,
- clinical, mechanistic, and translational ambitions stacked without discipline,
- validation burdens hidden inside secondary aims.

## Control Principles

- keep one central question,
- minimize dependencies,
- downgrade nice-to-have analyses to subordinate status,
- remove aims that require a different study identity,
- and prefer a smaller coherent package over a broader unstable one.

## Important Rule

A study can be ambitious or coherent. It should not pretend to be both when the evidence chain does not support it.
