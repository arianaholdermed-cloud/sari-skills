# Validation Evidence Hierarchy

Always separate discovery from stronger evidence layers.

## Evidence Layers
1. Discovery layer: primary dataset findings.
2. Internal robustness layer: covariate adjustment, sensitivity checks, resampling, or alternate preprocessing.
3. External support layer: independent cohort or public dataset replication.
4. Orthogonal support layer: alternate modality, assay, clinical variable alignment, or literature-supported mechanism.
5. Experimental / translational extension: wet-lab follow-up, targeted assay validation, or prospectively relevant confirmation.

## Rules
- One cohort is not enough for a strong translational claim.
- Pathway agreement is weaker than feature-level replication when feature-level replication is feasible.
- Deconvolution agreement is indirect support, not direct cell-state validation.
