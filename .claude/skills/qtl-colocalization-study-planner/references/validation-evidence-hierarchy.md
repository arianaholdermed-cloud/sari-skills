# Validation and Evidence Hierarchy

## Tier 1 — Locus Overlap Only
Signals are near each other, but shared-causal-signal support is not yet established.

## Tier 2 — Suggestive Shared-Signal Support
Primary coloc analysis supports a shared signal, but data limitations, tissue mismatch, or locus complexity remain important caveats.

## Tier 3 — Prioritized Shared-Signal Candidate
Shared-signal support is stronger and the locus-level candidate ranking is coherent with the molecular layer being studied.

## Tier 4 — Multi-Layer Convergent Support
The prioritized candidate is supported across more than one relevant evidence layer, such as additional QTL types, fine-mapping-compatible support, relevant annotation, or cautious MR/SMR follow-up.

## Downgrade Rules
Downgrade confidence when any of the following apply:
- ancestry mismatch or poor LD fit
- multiple independent signals are unresolved
- summary statistics are incomplete
- tissue / cell-type relevance is weak
- candidate ranking depends too heavily on prior literature instead of locus evidence

## Claim Rules
- Tier 1 does **not** support effector-gene claims.
- Tier 2 supports cautious locus-level prioritization language only.
- Tier 3 supports a prioritized candidate claim with explicit limitations.
- Tier 4 supports stronger follow-up prioritization, not absolute causal proof.
