# RWE Question Fit Rules

## Core Rule
Use an RWE study design when the question depends on longitudinal observation in routine-care data, treatment utilization, comparative effectiveness, safety, adherence, switching, or outcome occurrence under real practice conditions.

## Good-Fit Patterns
- Treatment initiation, switching, discontinuation, persistence, or adherence.
- Comparative effectiveness or safety under routine care.
- Longitudinal prognosis or healthcare-utilization outcomes.
- Device, procedure, or policy outcomes captured over follow-up.
- Registry follow-up or linked-data outcome assessment.

## Caution Patterns
- Pure mechanistic biology questions.
- Questions that require precise latent clinical constructs not plausibly captured in the proposed data source.
- Questions that are really diagnostic-accuracy or assay-validation studies.
- Questions that need trial-style randomization to be interpretable.

## Reporting Rule
Always state whether the question is mainly descriptive, associative, or causal-leaning before building the design.
