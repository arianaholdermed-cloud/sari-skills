# Validation and Evidence Hierarchy
# dual-disease-shared-transcriptome-biomarker-research-planner

---

## Evidence Tiers

### Tier 1 — Association / Discovery
Includes:
- per-disease differential expression
- same-direction overlap or concordance screening
- enrichment analysis
- PPI centrality ranking

**What it supports:**
- shared-signal discovery
- biological plausibility
- candidate prioritization

**What it does not support:**
- causality
- clinical utility by itself
- mechanistic confirmation

---

### Tier 2 — Diagnostic / Utility Support
Includes:
- ROC / AUC in disease A
- ROC / AUC in disease B
- expression direction validation in external cohorts

**What it supports:**
- preliminary biomarker utility
- stronger confidence in a selected endpoint

**What it does not support:**
- clinical deployment
- treatment guidance

---

### Tier 3 — Orthogonal Support
Includes:
- protein atlas or portal support
- tissue validation
- consistency across independent data modalities

**What it supports:**
- stronger plausibility and reviewer-facing confidence

**What it does not support:**
- definitive mechanism if functional evidence is absent

---

### Tier 4 — Functional / Mechanistic Support
Includes:
- perturbation experiments
- phenotype assays
- targeted mechanistic follow-up

**What it supports:**
- stronger biological plausibility around the chosen endpoint

**What it still may not support:**
- population-level clinical utility without clinical validation

---

## Required Output Labels

Every final plan should clearly label findings as one of:
- **Association evidence**
- **Diagnostic utility evidence**
- **Orthogonal support**
- **Hypothesis-generating mechanism context**

Do not merge these labels into a single claim.

---

## Reviewer-Facing Boundary Statements

Use language such as:
- "shared transcriptomic association"
- "candidate shared biomarker"
- "bulk-inferred immune context"
- "orthogonal plausibility support"
- "hypothesis-generating mechanistic interpretation"

Avoid language such as:
- "confirmed mechanism"
- "causal regulator"
- "clinically validated diagnostic marker"
- "proven therapeutic target"
