# Saturation Signal Framework

Use this module to identify whether a topic is truly crowded, strategically occupied, or only superficially noisy.

Saturation is not identical to popularity.
A topic can be highly visible but still weakly occupied at the evidence level.
A topic can also have modest publication volume but still be strategically saturated if the strongest claims are already taken.

## Primary Saturation Signals

Review the following:
- publication density in the exact topic unit
- repeated study-template density
- repeated signature / model / assay pattern density
- concentration of output among recurring major groups
- claim congestion around the same endpoint or framing
- comparator congestion
- validation-depth accumulation
- translational or implementation occupancy
- closure of obvious first-position claims

## Signal Interpretation Rules

### Suggests true saturation when:
- many studies address substantially the same question,
- major claim types are repeatedly occupied,
- validation depth has accumulated,
- and differentiated entry would require non-trivial repositioning.

### Suggests superficial crowding when:
- titles are numerous but designs are repetitive and weak,
- most work remains discovery-stage,
- validation is thin,
- or the field is noisy without strong closure of key claims.

### Suggests strategic saturation when:
- the numerically strongest area is not necessarily huge,
- but the most publishable or fundable positions are already occupied,
- benchmark claims are already established,
- and late entry would struggle to differentiate.

## Important Reporting Rule

Do not call a field saturated from publication count alone.
State which specific signals support the saturation judgment.
