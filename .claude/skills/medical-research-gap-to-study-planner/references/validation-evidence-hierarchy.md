# Validation and Evidence Hierarchy
# medical-research-gap-to-study-planner

---

## Evidence Tiers

### Tier 1. Discovery Evidence
Initial identification of an association, signal, candidate mechanism, or subgroup difference.

**Can support:**
- hypothesis generation
- candidate prioritization
- preliminary biological plausibility

**Cannot support:**
- robust generalization
- causality
- clinical implementation

---

### Tier 2. Internal Robustness Evidence
Sensitivity analyses, internal resampling, alternate parameter choices, or within-study robustness checks.

**Can support:**
- reduced fragility of the initial result

**Cannot support:**
- true external reproducibility

---

### Tier 3. External Validation Evidence
Independent cohort confirmation, orthogonal dataset confirmation, or cross-platform consistency.

**Can support:**
- stronger reproducibility claims
- improved confidence in generalizability

**Cannot support:**
- mechanism by itself
- implementation by itself

---

### Tier 4. Functional / Mechanistic Support
Perturbation evidence, pathway manipulation, mediator testing, tissue or cell functional support.

**Can support:**
- stronger biological interpretation
- narrowed mechanistic uncertainty

**Cannot support:**
- broad clinical utility unless clinical evidence is also present

---

### Tier 5. Translational Support
Clinically interpretable endpoints, patient subgroup utility, response stratification, or real-world feasibility anchors.

**Can support:**
- translational relevance claims
- stronger clinical framing

**Cannot support:**
- routine implementation without additional prospective or implementation evidence

---

## Output Requirement

For every protocol, explicitly state:
- which evidence tiers are generated,
- which tiers are absent,
- and which claims therefore remain out of scope.
