# Validation and Evidence Hierarchy
# single-drug-faers-safety-profile-research-planner

---

## Core Principle

FAERS-based studies provide different kinds of evidence. These must not be mixed.

### Tier 1 — Signal-Detection-Level Evidence
Supports statements such as:
- a drug or drug group is disproportionately reported with an event or event family
- a signal appears stronger or weaker under the declared signal metric

Does **not** support:
- causality
- incidence
- absolute or comparative clinical risk

### Tier 2 — Comparative / Characterization-Level Evidence
Supports statements such as:
- one restricted comparator frame yields stronger or weaker reporting signal
- a subgroup, seriousness, or onset pattern is observed inside the reporting data

Does **not** support:
- true biological susceptibility
- treatment effect differences
- regulatory action thresholds

### Tier 3 — Robustness-Level Evidence
Supports statements such as:
- the signal survives alternate filters, metrics, or restrictions
- the main story is less likely to be an artifact of one analytic choice

Does **not** support:
- external clinical validation
- mechanistic confirmation

### Tier 4 — Excluded Inference Zone
The following are outside the evidence ceiling of this skill family unless external data are added explicitly:
- definitive causal claims
- clinical incidence claims
- benefit-risk recommendations
- regulatory or prescribing conclusions

---

## Required Output Behavior

Every final plan must:
1. label each major result by evidence tier
2. state what the strongest validation layer proves
3. state what remains unvalidated
4. state which claims are forbidden because dependencies are absent
