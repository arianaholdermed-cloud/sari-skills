# Background-Gap-Objective Rules

## Core Rule
A strong introduction is not a list of known facts. It is a controlled progression from problem to gap to study objective.

## Background Should Do
- establish the problem,
- explain why it matters,
- narrow toward the most relevant unresolved limitation.

## Gap Should Do
- identify what is insufficient in current knowledge, evidence, method, or practice,
- remain tightly connected to the actual study,
- avoid being artificially inflated.

## Objective Should Do
- show how the present study addresses that gap,
- match the study design and evidence type,
- avoid promising more than the study can deliver.

## Important Rule
The gap must be specific enough to justify the study, but not broader than what the study can realistically address.
