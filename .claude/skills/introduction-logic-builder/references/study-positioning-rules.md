# Study Positioning Rules

## Core Rule
The introduction should position the study accurately, not theatrically.

## Position Around
- what kind of evidence the study provides,
- what limitation it addresses,
- why the chosen design is relevant,
- what contribution level is realistic.

## Avoid
- generic novelty claims,
- inflated disease-burden openings that never connect to the study,
- pretending exploratory work is definitive,
- implying translational readiness without support.

## Important Rule
A good positioning statement makes the paper’s role legible without overselling it.
