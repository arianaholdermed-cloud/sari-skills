# Ambiguity and Edge-Case Rules

## Core Rule
A good eligibility draft should anticipate disagreement before screening begins.

## Ambiguity signals
- terms like recent, active, severe, adequate, confirmed, clinically significant
- stage or subtype labels without defining evidence source
- treatment history without time window
- specimen requirement without timing or quality standard
- overlapping criteria that could be read in multiple ways
- criteria that depend on reviewer interpretation rather than evidence hierarchy

## Edge-case review should ask
- Would two reviewers classify the same record differently?
- Is the criterion unverifiable in some records?
- Does the criterion conflict with another part of the draft?
- Could this rule remove too many otherwise relevant patients?
- Is adjudication needed for borderline cases?
