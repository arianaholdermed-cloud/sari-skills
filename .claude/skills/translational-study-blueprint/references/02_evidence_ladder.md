# Evidence Ladder

## Core Rule
Separate evidence layers explicitly. Do not collapse them into a single statement of "support."

## Evidence Layers
1. Discovery association
2. Technical / assay validity
3. Biological or mechanistic support
4. Perturbational or intervention-linked support
5. Retrospective clinical association
6. Reproducibility across cohorts / settings
7. Prospective or externally staged validation
8. Clinical performance evidence at the intended decision point
9. Workflow, implementation, or real-world evidence

## Important Rule
Higher translational claims require higher evidence layers.

## Reporting Rule
For each evidence statement, specify whether it is:
- already available,
- partially available,
- required next,
- currently unsupported.

## Failure Pattern To Avoid
Do not describe mechanistic plausibility or retrospective association as if it already demonstrates clinical decision utility.
