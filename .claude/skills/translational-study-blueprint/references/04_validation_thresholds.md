# Validation Thresholds

## Core Rule
Every major stage needs an advancement threshold.

## Minimum Threshold Types
For each stage define:
- what minimal supportive evidence looks like,
- what stronger advancement-worthy evidence looks like,
- what would count as a non-advancement signal.

## Examples Of Threshold Logic
- Technical stage: acceptable repeatability, calibration stability, or inter-run consistency.
- Biological stage: signal consistency across models, tissues, or perturbations.
- Clinical association stage: reproducible direction and interpretable effect at the correct clinical endpoint.
- Performance stage: discrimination / calibration / stratification utility at the intended decision point.
- Translational advancement stage: evidence strong enough to justify external validation, prospective study, or development escalation.

## Reporting Rule
Avoid vague phrases such as "validate further." State what must be validated, why, and what result would change the plan.
