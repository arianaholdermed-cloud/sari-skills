# Confirmatory vs Exploratory Rules

Distinguish the posture of the objective before finalizing the wording.

## Confirmatory Objective
Use when the objective is primarily testing a defined relationship, comparison, or validation target.

## Exploratory Objective
Use when the objective is primarily discovery-oriented, pattern-finding, or hypothesis-generating.

## Mixed Objective
Use when the study has a clear primary aim but also contains exploratory secondary components.

## Important Rule
Do not label an objective as confirmatory just because the wording is formal. The study logic must support that posture.

## Failure Modes
- using confirmatory wording for discovery work
- hiding exploratory work inside a confirmatory objective
- avoiding the distinction entirely
