# Claim Discipline Rules
# result-reliability-checker

---

## Goal

This module prevents the skill from accepting paper conclusions at face value when the interpretation exceeds the evidence.

---

## Common Overclaim Patterns

### 1. Association -> Causation / Mechanism
The paper observes correlation or group difference but talks as if mechanism or causation were proven.

### 2. Retrospective Performance -> Clinical Utility
The paper shows retrospective model or biomarker performance and speaks as if clinical adoption is justified.

### 3. Subgroup Signal -> Stable Finding
The paper treats small or exploratory subgroup results as established.

### 4. Benchmark Win -> Broad Superiority
The paper outperforms one comparator or one dataset and generalizes the claim too widely.

### 5. Feasibility Signal -> Practice Readiness
The paper shows proof-of-concept but uses near-routine language.

---

## Output Rule

The final report must explicitly state:
- where the paper stays within the evidence,
- where it overclaims,
- and what the safer interpretation should be.
