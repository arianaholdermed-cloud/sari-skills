# Evidence Source Hierarchy

Use this module to prioritize evidence sources for unmet-need extraction.

Not all sources support unmet-need claims equally well. Strong extraction should favor sources that speak directly to clinical practice limitations or unresolved care decisions.

## Preferred Source Order

1. guidelines, consensus statements, and practice recommendations
2. major narrative reviews or systematic reviews that explicitly discuss unresolved care problems
3. real-world evidence, registries, and observational studies that show practical performance gaps or heterogeneity
4. original clinical studies that clarify specific limitations or unresolved decisions
5. preprints only as supplementary recency signals, clearly labeled

## Important Rules

- Do not rely on disease burden statistics alone.
- Do not treat technology-forward papers as unmet-need proof unless they explicitly show the clinical gap.
- Use real-world evidence carefully when it reflects routine-practice limitations.
- When the highest-priority sources do not support the stated need, downgrade confidence.
